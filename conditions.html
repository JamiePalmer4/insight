Common Eye Conditions

Explore common conditions which effect the eye, and how they have an effect upon millions of individuals around the world, including conditions like Cataracts, Glaucoma, and Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD). These conditions all have an effect in some way, with all very different compared to each other.

Refractive Errors

Refractive errors, including myopia and hyperopia, are common eye issues. According to the National Institute, around 150 million people could benefit by acquiring products like glasses and contacts, or undertake surgery.

Cataract

Cataract is ranked as one of the primary causes of blindness worldwide, where the eye lens clouds. Treatment options are available in order to improve the eye health, but if unattended to then total blindness may occur.

Diabetic Retinopathy

Resulting from diabetes complication, Diabetic Retinopathy is the leading cause of blindness amongst adults worldwide. It progresses through different stages, which if left untreated may cause significant risk to an individuals vision.

 

Glaucoma

Glaucoma comprises of a range of different eye diseases which can harm the optic nerve, heading towards vision impairment and total blindness. With Glaucoma being closely associated with elevated eye pressure, recent findings have found that it can occur even with normal pressure.

Amblyopia

Also known as "lazy eye", Amblyopia is the leading cause of vision impairment amongst children, reducing vision in one eye due to a lack of connection between the brain and the eye. Even though it appears normally, it is underused as it favours the other eye.

AMD

Age-related Macular Degeneration (AMD) is a eye condition relating to aging of an individual. This deteriorates the central vision of the eye, which effects daily tasks like driving and reading. This usually effects the macula, the central part of the eye responsible for acknowledging finer details.
