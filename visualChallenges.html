Daily Challenges of Visual Impairment

Leisure Activities

The visually impaired face challenges due to the lack of accessibility, impacting their access for leisure activities. Many options are limited in the leisure community, like museums, which struggle with providing visually impaired users with access, and reading materials also. In addition, a number of websites fail to cater for the needs of impaired users, effecting their viewing experience on websites.

Too Helpful Individuals

Sighted Individuals, whether they may be friends, family, or even a total stranger, tend to be too eager to assist an individual who has a visual impairment. This sense of eagerness gives off the assumption that the impaired individual requires help, eve though it may not be the case. This could stop their ability to learn new tasks as they are not undertaking them independently, making them feel reliant of help.

Finding a Job

Visually Impaired users often find it difficult locating a job due to unaccessible work environments. In addition, organisations see it as hiring a liability which further extends the challenge of acquiring a job. This overall puts the individuals self-confidence and independence level down, leaving them with no avenue to pursue and gain knowledge and experience, and ultimately support themselves in todays economy.

Assistive Technologies

Screen Readers

Screen Readers are vital assisitve technologies that output read aloud content displayed on screen, allowing those with impairments to access information. The users commonly use keyboard shortcuts like the 'tab' key, to navigate efficiently throughout pages. Also, screen readers integrate with braille display devices, communicating digital information into braille output. Screen Readers are compatible with various applications, such as VoiceOver, which is made by Apple, and Narrator, which is made by Windows.

Braille Displays

Through Bluetooth or USB connection, a braille display translates digital text shown on a screen into braille, enhancing the accessibility for blind and visually impaired users. With an integrated braille and QWERTY keyboards, these devices offer flexible options to impaired individuals to choose their preferred method.

Screen Magnification

Screen Magnification software was created in order to enlargen the size of text, images, and graphics displayed on screen, assisting users with impairments. In addition, it can invert colours to improve colour contrast for users. A number of devices offer magnification tools like the magnification software on Mac called Zoom, and a magnifier app on Windows called Windows Magnifier. Some of these softwares combine magnification with speech features, such as ZoomText Fusion and Supernova.
